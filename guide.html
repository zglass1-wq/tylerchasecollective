Welcome

You don't need to know
what to do.

If the thought of being photographed for 8 hours sounds a little terrifying. That's completely normal. Most couples feel that way. But you're not going to be posing in front of a camera all day. That's not how this works.

My job is to show up, blend in, and move quietly through your day while catching everything worth catching. I'll give you direction when you need it: where to walk, how to stand, what to do with your hands. Then I get out of the way so the day can actually happen around you.

This guide covers everything: packages, add-ons, albums, and every question I get asked. If something's not in here, just ask.

FAQs

Stuff you're probably wondering.

Do you travel for destination weddings?

For any wedding that requires a flight within the US, I just ask for a hotel room for 2 nights (3 nights if I'm covering the rehearsal dinner). For weddings over 100 miles away but drivable within 2 hours, I ask for a hotel the night before and the night of, mostly so you know I'm already in town and not racing against traffic on your wedding morning. For international weddings, an additional room may be needed depending on the situation. All travel (flights, hotel, rental car) is reimbursed after the wedding. I'll find good deals and invoice you after. Reach out for custom quotes on anything international.

What's your turnaround time?

One month for weddings. Two weeks for engagement sessions.

How do you deliver photos, and can we download them?

Through an online gallery that's easy to use. Everything is high resolution and fully edited. You can download straight to your phone or computer, make favorites lists, share with family and friends, and place print orders right from the gallery.

How many photos will we get?

Around 100 per hour for weddings. 150+ for engagement sessions.

Will all the photos be edited?

Yes, every one is fully color corrected. I also remove anything distracting: blemishes, trash on the ground, random people in the background who don't belong there.

Will you edit in different styles, like dark and moody?

I stick to my one style, and it's intentional. I want your photos to have continuity across the whole day. Not a mix of different looks depending on the light or the moment. My style is also timeless. Dark and moody has a shelf life. Yours won't.

How long will the gallery be available?

Technically forever. But I always recommend downloading everything immediately when you get it and backing it up to a hard drive. Don't leave it solely in the cloud.

Will you deliver RAW files?

Nope. You'd need professional software just to open them, and a huge part of my style comes from the editing. The finished images are what you're hiring me for.

Do you deliver black and whites?

Yes! I love a good B&W. Any photo I convert, you'll also get in color. I tend to make ones B&W that feel sentimental, romantic, or that I just love the composition of.

We're nervous about being in front of the camera. Will you direct us?

That's literally my whole thing. I don't expect you to know what to do. It's awkward, I completely get it. I use prompts and movement to get you out of your head and into the moment. Natural reactions. Not stiff, prom-style poses.

What should we do with our engagement photos?

A lot of things: save the dates, a guest book for the wedding, framed prints at the reception, thank you cards. I have discount codes for Minted and Basic Invite, both offer free address labeling, just ask. And please don't print at Snapfish or CVS. The colors never come out right.

We already had a photographer at the proposal. Can you discount the package?

Of course. Engagement sessions are a great practice run and a good way to get comfortable together before the wedding, but if you've already done one, I'll take $500 off your package. I'd still love to grab drinks or brunch beforehand so we can actually meet before your day.

What time should we be done with hair and makeup?

1 to 2.5 hours before you need to be anywhere, depending on whether you're doing a first look. More buffer is always better. You don't want to feel rushed right before your ceremony.

Do you bring your own reception lighting?

I use an on-camera flash only, so you don't have to worry about extra equipment being set up in the room or killing the vibe.

What kind of reception lighting looks best in photos?

String lights, always. If you want more of a party feel, ask your DJ to use white lights only. Colored lights, especially red or green, can look really strange in photos. Trust me on this one.

Why is there a discount when using an associate photographer?

Honestly, I'm bummed when I can't be there, so I discount the package as a kind of wedding gift from me to you. I also just want great photography to be available to more people.

Will the photos still look like yours if we use an associate?

Yes. I've trained my associates to shoot the way I do. But the biggest factor is editing. Every gallery comes back to me and gets edited in my style before it goes to you. So yes, they'll look like mine.
